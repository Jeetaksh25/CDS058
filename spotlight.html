SANDEEP JAIN

The CEO and founder of GeeksforGeeks is Sandeep Jain. Established in 2009, GeeksforGeeks has grown under his leadership to become a leading online education platform offering resources in programming, computer science, and interview preparation. Sandeep Jain’s journey includes facing setbacks, one of which was his failure to clear the JEE (Joint Entrance Examination), a highly competitive test for engineering colleges in India. This failure, however, motivated him to keep learning and later inspired him to help others who struggle with academics and technical skills. Sandeep Jain's journey from a software developer to the founder of GeeksforGeeks showcases the power of passion and vision. Despite having a successful career at D.E. Shaw, he shifted to teaching, driven by a desire to make quality coding education accessible. Starting as a blog to share programming insights, GeeksforGeeks has grown into a global edtech platform under his leadership, providing articles, practice problems, and courses that support millions of learners worldwide.

SUNDAR PICHAI

Sundar Pichai, born Pichai Sundararajan in 1972 in Madurai, India, is the CEO of Alphabet Inc. and its subsidiary, Google LLC. Growing up in a modest family in Chennai, he excelled in academics, earning a degree in Metallurgical Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur . Pichai then pursued an M.S. from Stanford University and an MBA from the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania. He joined Google in 2004, initially working on the Google Toolbar and later the Chrome browser, which became a massive success. His innovative contributions led to his appointment as Google's CEO in 2015, and in 2019, he became the CEO of Alphabet as well. Known for his strategic vision, humility, and focus on technological advancement, Pichai has played a key role in developing products like Google Chrome, Chrome OS, and Android, as well as in leading Google’s efforts in AI and machine learning.

Dhirubhai Ambani

Dhirubhai Ambani, born in 1932 in Gujarat, began his journey in Yemen as a gas station attendant. Returning to India in 1958, he founded Reliance as a small textile trading firm. Starting with limited capital, he grew Reliance into a leading company, pioneering affordable textiles with the "Vimal" brand. Dhirubhai revolutionized Indian business by making Reliance one of the first public companies, attracting everyday investors. He later expanded into petrochemicals, plastics, and telecom, building Reliance into a powerhouse. Known for his vision and resilience, he inspired a new wave of Indian entrepreneurship.

Mark Zuckerberg

Born on May 14, 1984, in White Plains, New York, Zuckerberg showed an early aptitude for programming and technology. As a student at Harvard University, he developed “The Facebook” in 2004 with friends Eduardo Saverin, Andrew McCollum, Dustin Moskovitz, and Chris Hughes, initially as a social network for college students. The platform quickly gained popularity and expanded beyond universities, transforming into a global phenomenon. Zuckerberg dropped out of Harvard to focus on Facebook full-time, relocating to Silicon Valley.

Narendra Modi

Narendra Modi’s journey is a story of perseverance and ambition, rising from humble beginnings to lead India as its Prime Minister. Born on September 17, 1950, in Vadnagar, Gujarat, Modi helped his family by selling tea at a railway station in his early years. He joined the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS) as a young man.Through his hard work, Modi advanced within the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP), eventually becoming Chief Minister of Gujarat in 2001. In 2014, Modi ran for Prime Minister on a platform of economic development, modernization, and anti-corruption, leading the BJP to a historic victory Since then, he has faced multiple challenges but has remained focused on economic reforms, international relations, and initiatives like "Make in India" and "Digital India".
